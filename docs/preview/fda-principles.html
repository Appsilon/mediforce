10 Guiding Principles of Good AI Practice in Drug Development

In January 2026, FDA and EMA jointly published 10 guiding principles for AI in drug development. Below is our plan for how Mediforce addresses each principle — what we've built so far, and where we're heading. We welcome your feedback as we work toward this together.

01 Human-centric by design
The development and use of AI technologies align with ethical and human-centric values.
Autonomy levels L0–L4 per workflow stepValidated AI → Control HITL enforcement — workflow pauses until human approvesValidated AI → Control Anti-rubber-stamping — review quality monitoringValidated AI → Watch
02 Risk-based approach
The development and use of AI technologies follow a risk-based approach with proportionate validation, risk mitigation, and oversight based on the context of use and determined model risk.
Proportional rigor per use caseValidation Confidence thresholds with auto-escalationValidated AI → Control Autonomy level selection based on step riskValidated AI → Control
03 Adherence to standards
AI technologies adhere to relevant legal, ethical, technical, scientific, cybersecurity, and regulatory standards, including Good Practices (GxP).
Audit trail — 21 CFR Part 11 alignedValidated AI → Prove RBAC, access control, e-signaturesSecurity → Access AES-256-GCM encryption, namespace isolationSecurity → Encrypt
04 Clear context of use
AI technologies have a well-defined context of use (role and scope for why it is being used).
Workflow definitions with explicit step purposeValidated AI → Control Per-step agent config — model, prompt, tools scopedSecurity → Isolate
05 Multidisciplinary expertise
Multidisciplinary expertise covering both the AI technology and its context of use are integrated throughout the technology's life cycle.
Role-based step assignment — different experts per stepValidated AI → Control Per-step RBAC — right expert reviews right stepSecurity → Access Producer ≠ reviewer — enforced separation of dutiesValidated AI → Control
06 Data governance & documentation
Data source provenance, processing steps, and analytical decisions are documented in a detailed, traceable, and verifiable manner, in line with GxP requirements.
Audit trail with input/output snapshotsValidated AI → Prove Decision reconstruction — full chain from input to approvalValidated AI → Prove Per-step data scoping — agents see only authorized dataSecurity → Isolate
07 Model design & development
The development of AI technologies follows best practices in model and system design, considering interpretability, explainability, and predictive performance. Good development promotes transparency, reliability, and robustness.
Agent output envelope — confidence, reasoning chain, annotationsValidated AI → Control Model-agnostic — any model, self-hosted or cloudSecurity → Deploy Open source — inspect every lineGitHub
08 Risk-based performance assessment
Risk-based performance assessments evaluate the complete system including human-AI interactions, using fit-for-use data and metrics appropriate for the intended context of use.
Agent benchmark suites — run N times, measure qualityValidated AI → Test End-to-end workflow evalsValidated AI → Test Judge agents — AI reviewing AI outputsValidated AI → Watch
09 Life cycle management
Risk-based quality management systems are implemented throughout the AI technologies' life cycles. The AI technologies undergo scheduled monitoring and periodic re-evaluation to ensure adequate performance (e.g., to address data drift).
Agent version tracking — model, prompt, config per executionValidated AI → Prove Drift monitoring — periodic benchmark re-runsValidated AI → Test Qualification reports — auditor-ready documentationValidated AI → Prove
10 Clear, essential information
Plain language is used to present clear, accessible, and contextually relevant information regarding the AI technology's context of use, performance, limitations, and interpretability.
Reasoning chain & confidence rationale in every outputValidated AI → Control Workflow risk report — one view of how risk is managedValidated AI → Prove

About this page

The Guiding Principles of Good AI Practice in Drug Development was published jointly by the U.S. FDA and the European Medicines Agency in January 2026.

This page is a request for comment — our evolving design blueprint for how Mediforce intends to address each principle. Status reflects where we are today in that process, not a finished compliance claim. We welcome feedback as we iterate.

Mediforce is open source — every feature listed here can be inspected in the codebase. If you have thoughts on how we're approaching these principles, join us on Discord.
